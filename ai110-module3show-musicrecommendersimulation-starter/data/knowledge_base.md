# VibeFinder Music Knowledge Base

This document supplements the song catalog with domain knowledge about genres,
moods, and listening contexts. VibeFinder uses it to give richer, more accurate
explanations — especially when the user's query doesn't map cleanly to catalog labels.

---

## Genre Profiles

### Lofi Hip-Hop
Tempo: 60–85 BPM · Energy: 0.25–0.50 · Usually instrumental or minimal vocals
Characterized by: filtered, lo-fidelity audio; ambient noise layers (rain, coffee shop);
minor keys; slow-rolling drum samples; melancholy-but-comforting atmosphere.
Best for: studying, late-night work sessions, relaxed focus, background listening.
Mood associations: chill, focused, calm, nostalgic, dreamy.
Catalog examples: Midnight Coding, Library Rain, Focus Flow.

### Pop
Tempo: 100–135 BPM · Energy: 0.60–0.92 · Vocals-forward, hook-driven
Characterized by: polished production, wide dynamic range, relatable lyrics,
strong verse-chorus structure, danceable rhythms.
Best for: commuting, social settings, upbeat daily tasks, dancing.
Mood associations: happy, confident, uplifting, energetic.
Catalog examples: Sunrise City, Gym Hero.

### Ambient
Tempo: 50–75 BPM · Energy: 0.10–0.40 · Often fully instrumental
Characterized by: texture over rhythm, slow evolution, atmospheric synthesizer pads,
reverb-heavy soundscapes, no conventional song structure.
Best for: meditation, deep focus, sleep, background listening, stress relief.
Mood associations: peaceful, spacious, meditative, dreamy, chill.
Catalog examples: Spacewalk Thoughts.

### Rock
Tempo: 120–160 BPM · Energy: 0.70–0.95 · Guitar-and-drums-driven
Characterized by: distorted or clean electric guitar, prominent drum kit,
structured song forms (verse-chorus-bridge), often energetic vocals.
Best for: driving, high-energy tasks, workouts, releasing tension.
Mood associations: intense, powerful, driving, confident.
Catalog examples: Storm Runner.

### Jazz
Tempo: 80–130 BPM · Energy: 0.30–0.60 · Improvisation-forward
Characterized by: complex harmonies, extended chords, acoustic instruments
(upright bass, piano, brushed drums), improvised solos, swing feel.
Best for: dinner background, creative work, relaxed social settings, Sunday mornings.
Mood associations: relaxed, sophisticated, warm, nostalgic, smooth.
Catalog examples: Coffee Shop Stories.

### Synthwave
Tempo: 100–130 BPM · Energy: 0.60–0.85 · Electronic, 80s-inspired
Characterized by: analog synthesizers, driving bass lines, gated reverb drums,
cinematic textures, retro-futuristic aesthetic.
Best for: driving at night, focused creative work, gaming.
Mood associations: moody, nocturnal, driving, nostalgic.
Catalog examples: Night Drive Loop.

### Indie Pop
Tempo: 110–130 BPM · Energy: 0.60–0.82 · Alternative production style
Characterized by: jangly guitars, introspective lyrics, less polished than mainstream
pop, experimental song structures, emphasis on mood and texture.
Best for: walks, creative work, social settings, low-key parties.
Mood associations: happy, uplifting, bright, danceable.
Catalog examples: Rooftop Lights.

### Hip-Hop
Tempo: 80–100 BPM · Energy: 0.65–0.85 · Beat-driven, lyric-focused
Characterized by: rhythmic speech-based vocals, sample-based or synthesized beats,
strong bass presence, emphasis on wordplay and flow.
Best for: commuting, gym warm-up, confident daily routines.
Mood associations: confident, bold, energetic, swagger.
Catalog examples: Corner Store Flex.

### R&B
Tempo: 80–110 BPM · Energy: 0.45–0.70 · Smooth, soulful
Characterized by: melismatic vocals, smooth chord progressions, emphasis on groove
and feel, romantic or emotional lyrical themes.
Best for: intimate evenings, dates, relaxed social settings.
Mood associations: romantic, smooth, warm, soulful.
Catalog examples: Velvet Overtime.

### Metal
Tempo: 140–200 BPM · Energy: 0.88–1.00 · Maximum intensity
Characterized by: heavily distorted guitars, blast-beat or double-kick drums,
aggressive vocals (screamed or shouted), down-tuned instruments.
Best for: intense workouts, stress release, high-adrenaline gaming.
Mood associations: angry, aggressive, powerful, raw.
Catalog examples: Iron Collapse.

### Country
Tempo: 90–120 BPM · Energy: 0.40–0.65 · Storytelling, acoustic-forward
Characterized by: acoustic or steel guitar, narrative lyrics, twang vocal style,
themes of rural life, relationships, nostalgia.
Best for: road trips, backyard gatherings, nostalgic listening.
Mood associations: nostalgic, warm, storytelling, uplifting.
Catalog examples: Dusty Back Roads.

### Reggae
Tempo: 75–95 BPM · Energy: 0.55–0.75 · Offbeat rhythm, laid-back feel
Characterized by: guitar and keyboard on the offbeat (skank), prominent bass,
Jamaican musical influences, themes of positivity and social commentary.
Best for: summer listening, relaxed outdoor settings, morning routines.
Mood associations: uplifting, sunny, rhythmic, positive.
Catalog examples: Island Morning.

### Blues
Tempo: 65–100 BPM · Energy: 0.35–0.55 · Soulful, expressive
Characterized by: 12-bar chord progressions, expressive guitar bends, call-and-response
vocal style, emotional rawness, themes of hardship and resilience.
Best for: reflective listening, rainy days, emotional processing.
Mood associations: melancholic, soulful, raw, introspective.
Catalog examples: Rainy Tuesday Blues.

### Funk
Tempo: 100–125 BPM · Energy: 0.75–0.92 · Groove-first, syncopated
Characterized by: tight rhythmic interplay between bass, drums, and rhythm guitar,
syncopated "pocket" grooves, danceable feel, call-and-response arrangements.
Best for: dancing, parties, energetic social settings, cooking.
Mood associations: groovy, danceable, funky, upbeat.
Catalog examples: Groove Cabinet.

### Classical
Tempo: varies widely · Energy: 0.10–0.45 · Orchestral or chamber
Characterized by: acoustic instruments only, formal compositional structure,
wide dynamic range from pianissimo to fortissimo, no percussion-driven beat.
Best for: deep focus, studying, meditation, emotional reflection, sleep.
Mood associations: peaceful, elegant, serene, melancholic.
Catalog examples: Moonlit Sonata.

## Mood Psychology

### Chill / Relaxed
The listener wants to decompress without active mental engagement.
→ Prioritize: low energy (0.20–0.50), high acousticness, slow tempos.
→ Best genres: lofi, ambient, jazz, classical.

### Focused / Studying
Sustained attention without distraction. Lyrics compete with reading/writing.
→ Prioritize: instrumental or low-vocal tracks, moderate energy (0.30–0.55).
→ Best genres: lofi, classical, ambient.

### Happy / Uplifting
Positive emotional state or desire to elevate mood.
→ Prioritize: high valence, danceable rhythms, energetic production.
→ Best genres: pop, funk, reggae, indie pop.

### Intense / Angry
High arousal — gym, stress release, competitive gaming.
→ Prioritize: maximum energy (0.80+), driving rhythms, aggressive timbre.
→ Best genres: rock, metal, hip-hop.

### Romantic / Intimate
Soft attention on another person; background ambiance for connection.
→ Prioritize: moderate energy (0.45–0.65), warm acoustics, smooth production.
→ Best genres: r&b, jazz, indie pop.

### Nostalgic / Reflective
Looking inward, processing memories or emotions.
→ Prioritize: acoustic warmth, minor keys, storytelling genres.
→ Best genres: country, blues, classical, jazz.

### Moody / Nocturnal
Neither happy nor sad; late-night introspection or cinematic feel.
→ Prioritize: synthwave, lofi, ambient, moody r&b.

## How to Interpret Energy Scores

| Range | Description | Example feel |
|---|---|---|
| 0.00–0.30 | Very calm, almost ambient | whispered, spacious |
| 0.30–0.50 | Mellow — good for background | lofi study beat |
| 0.50–0.70 | Medium — engaging, not overwhelming | casual pop, jazz |
| 0.70–0.85 | High — clearly energetic and driving | upbeat pop, funk |
| 0.85–1.00 | Maximum intensity — aggressive, loud | metal, gym anthems |

When a user says...           → interpret as energy ≈
"something to wake me up"     → 0.75+
"something in the background" → ≤ 0.45
"chill"                       → ≤ 0.50
"calm" or "peaceful"          → ≤ 0.35
"intense" or "powerful"       → 0.85+
"upbeat" or "danceable"       → 0.65–0.85

## Scoring Signal Reference

When explaining a recommendation, reference the specific signals that fired:

| Signal | Fired when | Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| Genre match | song.genre == user.genre (exact) | +2.0 — strongest signal |
| Mood match | song.mood == user.mood (exact) | +1.0 |
| Energy fit | near-perfect: \|Δ\| < 0.2 | +0.8–1.0 |
| Energy fit | decent: \|Δ\| = 0.2–0.5 | +0.5–0.8 |
| Energy mismatch | \|Δ\| > 0.5 | < 0.5 — flag this honestly |
| Acoustic warmth | likes_acoustic=True, acousticness ≥ 0.6 | +0.5 |
| Era match | preferred_decade == song.release_decade | +0.25 |
| Mood tag hit | each matching tag (max 3) | +0.10 each |
| Popularity fit | close to popularity_target | up to +0.20 |
| Instrumental | wants_instrumental, instrumentalness ≥ 0.70 | +0.25 |
